Learning product development process

Getting started

The phases of this process provide a suggested template. You may start at different phases, and you may revisit phases as needed. The required steps will be highlighted.

Special note

This process is currently under review. We are working with our internal partners to continuously improve and optimize the way the School designs and delivers its learning experiences. Please contact us if you are an employee of the School and are interested in contributing: csps.edtechresearchinnovation-rechercheinnovationteched.efpc@canada.ca 

Phase 1 - Product initiation

This is the very beginning of the product development process. At this point, you have identified your business gap and are collecting questions for your learner and stakeholder research.

All learning products require you to fill out a Product Information Form.

Key partners: Curriculum management, LearningLab, and GCcampus

Phase 2 - Conduct user research

In this phase, your goal is to better understand the needs of your learners and stakeholders. Different research techniques will help you get there.

Key partners: LearningLab and Evaluation

Phase 3 - Develop instructional design (ID) plan 

An ID plan contains all insights you've collected through research as well as your plan to close the business gap through learning. Consulting with the LearningLab team will provide you with an opportunity to consider the possibilities at your disposal for generating solutions.

Key partners: LearningLab

Phase 4 - Build prototypes

Now that you have defined the problems, it's time to prototype solutions. These can be as simple as paper storyboards, or as refined as digital copies.

Don't be afraid to create a few prototypes for testing in the next phase, but please ensure that you've planned to use a technology that conforms with the approved tools.

Key partners: LearningLab, EdTech Research and Innovation, Accessibility, GCcampus

Phase 5 - Test solutions with users

We recommend you test your prototypes with your learners before going to full scale launch. This will help ensure that your experiences are inclusive, usable and accessible.

Key partners: GCcampus, Accessibility and Evaluation

Phase 6 - Prepare for product launch

This is the phase in the process where you've ensure that your solution meets all of the requirements, including usability, accessibility and reporting.

Key partners: GCcampus, Accessibility, Curriculum Management, Points of Contact and Communications

Phase 7 - Publish approved solution

Your product is launched.

Key partners: GCcampus, Curriculum Management, Points of Contact and Communications

Phase 8 - Monitor learner activity and feedback

In this phase, you are monitoring the learner traffic and feedback from your solution to ensure that there are no issues with the performance of the product and that all necessary changes are tracked and integrated.

Key partners: GCcampus, Client Contact Centre, Evaluation

Phase 9 - Share your content and course materials

This phase is optional, but we encourage you to share your course materials and other content in a reusable format so that others may benefit from your hard work.

Key partners: EdTech Research and Innovation
